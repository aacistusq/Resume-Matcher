My goals:
1. Make the scoring stricter and more believable

PART 1 — NEW SCORING PHILOSOPHY
==================================================

Do NOT let the LLM directly invent one final score from overall vibes.

Instead use a 2-step process:

Step 1:
The AI should analyze the JD and resume and return structured findings only.

Step 2:
The backend should calculate the actual score deterministically from those findings using fixed weights, penalties, and caps.

The score must be conservative.
If a resume is only loosely related, it should usually fall in the 40–65 range, not the 70s.

Target score interpretation:
- 85–100 = strong fit
- 70–84 = decent fit
- 50–69 = partial fit
- 30–49 = weak fit
- 0–29 = poor fit

Only give 80+ if there is strong evidence across most core requirements.

- key_responsibilities
- qualifications_or_certifications
- tools_and_technologies

Then the AI must compare the resume against each requirement and classify every item as:

- missing

- matched = direct and clear evidence in resume

- missing = no explicit evidence
- do not assume skills or tools unless clearly shown
- broad similarity is not enough for a full match
- transferable skills can count as partial, not matched
- each matched or partial item must include exact resume evidence text

PART 4 — STRICT AI SYSTEM PROMPT
==================================================

Replace the current analysis prompt with this stricter prompt:

You are a strict ATS resume scoring analyst.

Your task is to compare a candidate's resume against a job description and return structured evidence-based analysis.

Be conservative and skeptical.
Do not reward general similarity unless there is direct evidence in the resume.
Do not infer tools, technologies, or responsibilities unless explicitly mentioned or strongly evidenced.
If a requirement is only somewhat reflected, classify it as partial, not matched.
If there is no clear evidence, classify it as missing.

Scoring intent:
- Weak or loosely related resumes should often land in the 30–55 range after backend scoring
- Partial but somewhat relevant resumes should often land in the 50–69 range
- Strong resumes with clear evidence across most must-haves can land in the 70–84 range
- Only exceptional, highly aligned resumes should land in the 85+ range

Important rules:
1. For every matched or partial requirement, provide exact evidence from the resume
2. If there is no direct evidence, do not mark as matched
3. Broad business analytics experience does not automatically equal domain expertise
4. Broad data experience does not automatically mean Python, dbt, Snowflake, experimentation, or stakeholder leadership
5. Missing critical requirements should be clearly reflected in the analysis
6. Be strict about seniority mismatch
7. Be strict about lack of quantified achievements
8. Be strict about vague or generic resume bullets
9. Do not produce a final score
10. Return valid JSON only in the required schema

PART 5 — BACKEND DETERMINISTIC SCORING FORMULA
==================================================

Implement the final score in backend code, not by the LLM.

Use this exact model:

A. Must-have requirements = 40 points
B. Preferred requirements = 15 points
C. Experience relevance = 20 points
D. Evidence strength = 15 points
E. Resume quality / ATS clarity = 10 points

Status values:
- matched = 1.0
- partial = 0.5
- missing = 0.0

Section scoring:
- must-have section score = weighted coverage scaled to 40
- preferred section score = weighted coverage scaled to 15

For experience_relevance_score:
Allow AI to return a number from 0 to 20

For evidence_strength_score:
Allow AI to return a number from 0 to 15 based on:
- quantified achievements
- specificity of accomplishments
- strength of ownership language
- proof of tools and technologies
- measurable business outcomes

For resume_quality_score:
Allow AI to return a number from 0 to 10 based on:
- clarity
- structure
- ATS readability
- good formatting
- lack of fluff
- concise bullet quality
